About Me:

I wear many hats, but they all coordinate. Or maybe that's just my... unique... sense of fashion speaking. Above all, I am an archaeologist and educator. I've trained and worked in archaeology in various parts of the world since 2001.

In 2015, I graduated with my PhD from Michigan State University, where I researched some of the earliest farming settlements in the Kalahari Desert of Botswana. In the past few years I've also worked with museum-based informal education programs to spread the word about the importance of preserving the past. Most recently, I became the Transportation Review Coordinator for the South Carolina State Historic Preservation Office. I provide guidance and feedback on DOT construction projects around the state in terms of how they'll impact cultural resources - specifically, archaeological sites and historic structures.

I love this line of work because it lets me apply my academic training directly to real-world problems. Like most academically-oriented folks, I have several side projects going as well (because who needs free time, right?). I currently teach online courses for Baker College, a non-profit institution based in Michigan, and I'm writing up a number of publications. I'll try and keep this updated as my adventures continue!
