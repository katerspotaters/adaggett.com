Mission Statement:

-Adrianne is a PhD candidate in the Department of Anthropology at Michigan State University, specializing in anthropological archaeology. Her dissertation research focuses on early (ca 1000 AD) farming settlements in the Kalahari Desert of Botswana and the way they adopted the social and economic traditions of their time period to their local environment. This study involves analysis of spatial and social models, compiling multiple lines of dietary and technological evidence, and building a comparative review of sites contemporary to the ones she is researching. In 2012 Adrianne led a team of archaeologists on a three-month excavation project to gather the data for this research at three sites near Mosu Village, Botswana, near the Makgadikgadi Pans.
